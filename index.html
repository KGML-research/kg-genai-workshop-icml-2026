Proposed Workshop on

Incorporating Scientific Knowledge in Generative AI

ICML 2026 (Tentative) · July 10-11, Seoul, South Korea

Overview

While recent advances in generative AI and foundation models offer tremendous potential for accelerating scientific discovery, current methods still suffer from several limitations hindering their widespread adoption in science. This includes their inability to generalize in out-of-distribution scenarios (critical for extrapolation in scientific problems), tendency to hallucinate and produce physically inconsistent samples, and lack of interpretability and control required for scientific reasoning. We explore the question: how can we augment generative models with scientific knowledge to produce outputs that not only "look realistic" but are also scientifically valid, reliable, and useful for scientific discovery?

Workshop Goals: We aim to bring together the cross-disciplinary community of researchers working in generative AI and science with a common vision: integrating scientific knowledge directly into the foundations and frameworks of generative AI. Our focus is not merely on using GenAI to advance science, but also on how scientific knowledge itself can inform core GenAI innovations. We aim to bridge the gap between current capabilities of generative models and the needs of problems in scientific domains by standardizing problem formulations, benchmarks, evaluation protocols, and metrics, driving the next frontiers of generative AI for science.

The workshop will feature keynote talks, paper presentations, and panel discussions on emerging research at the intersection of GenAI and science. We encourage submissions aligned with the Call For Papers. The workshop will also provide opportunities for networking and collaboration.

This workshop is a continuation of our previous efforts in building the knowledge-guided machine learning (KGML) research community, including KGML Bridge programs at AAAI, KGML Fall Symposiums at AAAI, and KGML workshops at University of Minnesota. Our proposed workshop has a targeted focus on the role of knowledge guidance in generative AI and we aim to nurture the community of researchers working in this space across disciplines.
